# Classic Sourdough Bread

**Recipe Owner:** Kristine  
**Serves/Yield:** 2 loaves (about 24 slices)  
**Prep time:** 30 min (active)  
**Cook time:** 48-51 min per loaf  
**Estimated macros (est.):** ~170 cal | 6g protein | 0g fat | 35g carbs (per slice, about 24 slices)

### Ingredients
- 300 g sourdough starter
- 20 g salt (Redmond real salt is close to 2 tsp)
- 600 g water
- 900 g flour (half all-purpose, half bread flour)

### Instructions
1. Feed the starter: mix your starter with 1 cup flour and an equal weight of water. Leave it on the counter for about 4 hours, or until doubled in size and active.
2. Measure and mix: in a bowl, combine starter, salt, water, and flour. Mix with a dough hook for about 5 minutes. Cover the bowl.
3. Stretch and fold: after a few minutes, wet your hand, pull the dough up from the side, and fold toward the center. Rotate the bowl a quarter turn and repeat. Do this at least twice within the first hour, 5-10 minutes apart.
4. Bulk rise: let dough rise 6-8 hours, until roughly doubled. Don’t let it over-proof and start to collapse.
5. Divide and shape: turn dough onto a floured surface, divide in half, and shape each half into a tight ball. Line two Dutch ovens with parchment, or bake one loaf at a time, and place each dough ball inside to rise.
6. Second rise: let the loaves rise about 2 hours (longer if cool, shorter if warm).
7. Preheat: heat oven as hot as it will go (about 500°F), then reduce to 420°F.
8. Score: score the top of each loaf with a sharp knife, razor, or lame.
9. Bake: bake each loaf in a Dutch oven at 420°F for 45 minutes. Remove from the Dutch oven and bake directly on the rack 3-6 minutes more to brown the crust.

- This recipe works well in a 4-5 quart Dutch oven.
