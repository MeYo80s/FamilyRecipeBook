# Classic Sourdough Bread

**Recipe Owner:** Kristine  
**Serves/Yield:** 2 loaves (about 24 slices)  
**Prep time:** 30 min (active)  
**Cook time:** 48-51 min per loaf  
**Estimated macros (est.):** ~170 cal | 6g protein | 0g fat | 35g carbs (per slice, about 24 slices)

### Ingredients
- 300 g sourdough starter
- 20 g salt (Redmond real salt is close to 2 tsp)
- 600 g water
- 900 g bread flour

### Tools
- Kitchen scale
- Jar scraper
- Bowl scraper
- KitchenAid with dough hook, or equivalent mixer
- Wide-mouth jar for starter with a loose lid
- Dutch oven or bread pans
- Lame, razor, or scissors for scoring
- Parchment paper
- Bannetons (optional; you can also do the second rise in Dutch ovens or bread pans)

### Instructions
1. Feed the starter: mix your starter with 1 cup all-purpose flour and an equal weight of water. Leave it on the counter for about 4 hours, or until doubled in size and active.
2. Measure and mix: in a bowl, combine starter, salt, water, and flour. Mix with a dough hook for about 5 minutes. Cover the bowl.
3. Stretch and fold: after a few minutes, wet your hand, pull the dough up from the side, and fold toward the center. Rotate the bowl a quarter turn and repeat. Do this at least twice within the first hour, 5-10 minutes apart.
4. Bulk rise: let dough rise 6-8 hours, until roughly doubled. Don’t let it over-proof and start to collapse.
5. Divide and shape: turn dough onto a floured surface, divide in half, and shape each half into a tight ball. Line two Dutch ovens with parchment, or bake one loaf at a time, and place each dough ball inside to rise.
6. Second rise: let the loaves rise about 2 hours at room temperature, or refrigerate for at least 24 hours. If refrigerated, take them out about 1 hour before baking.
7. Preheat: heat oven as hot as it will go (about 500°F), then reduce to 420°F.
8. Score: score the top of each loaf with a sharp knife, razor, lame, or scissors.
9. Bake: bake each loaf in a Dutch oven at 420°F for 45 minutes. Remove from the Dutch oven and bake directly on the rack 3-6 minutes more to brown the crust.

### Family Notes
- I took this sourdough class with my sister-in-law Lindy.
- I use all-purpose flour to feed the starter, but bread flour for the dough.
- You only need a small amount of starter to maintain it, about 1 inch up the side of a wide-mouth mason jar.
- I keep my starter in the fridge and feed it about 1/4 cup each week if I am not baking with it.
- After adding starter to the dough, I usually feed the jar with about 1/4 cup flour and water before putting it back in the fridge. I think of it like feeding your kid before bedtime so it does not get hungry right away.
- I often skip the stretch-and-fold step and do not notice much difference either way.
- For the second rise, I usually refrigerate the loaves for at least 24 hours. After that, I score and bake them, usually after about 1 hour on the counter.
- I have also used this recipe for pizza dough. Use about 200 g dough per dough ball for a 12-inch pizza.
- You can bake in whatever vessel you have. Glass bread pans with another glass bread pan inverted on top work well.
- This recipe works well in a 4-5 quart Dutch oven.
- You can reuse parchment paper 3-4 times if you want.
